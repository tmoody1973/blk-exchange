# Sector Performance with Real Market Comparison

## For Teachers and Financial Literacy Programs

BLK Exchange now includes a sector performance tool that connects the simulated market to real Wall Street data. Here is how it works and why it matters for your students.

## What Students See

On the market page, below the news feed, there is a Sector Performance section showing all 12 sectors of the BLK Exchange economy. Each sector displays a color-coded daily change. Green means the sector is up. Red means down.

When a student taps any sector, a detailed panel opens with three sections:

### 1. The Sim Stocks

The three BLK Exchange companies in that sector, with current prices and daily performance. For example, tapping Finance shows:

- VAULT (community bank): $51.97, -0.06%
- STAX (fintech): $67.79, -0.31%
- GROW (CDFI lending): $28.67, -1.14%

Students can see at a glance which company in a sector is performing best and which is lagging. This teaches relative performance within a sector.

### 2. The Real ETF

Each BLK Exchange sector is mapped to a real Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) that tracks similar companies on Wall Street. The panel shows:

- The ETF name, ticker, and current real market price
- The daily change percentage from actual market data
- A side-by-side comparison bar showing how the sim sector and the real ETF moved today
- The top 5 real companies inside the ETF

For example, the Finance sector maps to XLF (Financial Select Sector SPDR), which holds JPMorgan Chase, Berkshire Hathaway, Visa, Mastercard, and Bank of America. Students see that when their sim Finance sector drops, real Financials often move in a similar direction. That connection is sector correlation, and they learn it by seeing it, not by reading about it.

### 3. Plain-Language Explanation

Every sector has a short paragraph explaining what the ETF is, what kind of companies it holds, and how it connects to the sim tickers the student already knows. No jargon. No assumptions about prior knowledge.

## What This Teaches

### Sector Correlation
When one sneaker company does well, other sneaker companies usually follow. Students see this pattern playing out in both the sim and real markets at the same time.

### Diversification
The sector chart shows which sectors are up and which are down on any given day. A student with all their money in Gaming sees the whole position drop together. A student spread across 6 sectors sees some green and some red. The chart makes diversification visual.

### ETFs and Index Investing
Every sector dropdown explains what an ETF is in plain language: a basket of stocks you buy with one purchase. Students learn that instead of picking one company, they could own a piece of every company in a sector. This is the concept that leads to the NACP capstone in The Real Exchange.

### Real Market Awareness
Students see actual market data alongside their sim. When they watch XLF drop 0.9% on the same day their sim Finance sector drops 1.2%, the game stops being abstract. The connection between the classroom and Wall Street becomes concrete.

## How to Use This in a Classroom

### Discussion Prompt
"Look at the sector chart. Which sectors are up today and which are down? Now tap one of the green sectors and one of the red sectors. What do the real ETFs show? Are the real markets moving in the same direction as the sim? What does that tell you about how sectors work?"

### Assignment Idea
Have each student pick a different sector. Over one week, they track both their sim sector performance and the real ETF performance. At the end of the week, they present: Did the two move together? Why or why not? What news events drove the changes?

### Assessment Connection
The sector chart directly supports the Knowledge Vault concepts of Sector Correlation, Diversification, and Supply & Demand. Students who engage with the sector comparison are building the vocabulary they need for those vault concepts to unlock through gameplay.

## Technical Notes

Real ETF data comes from Finnhub (free tier, 60 API calls per minute). Prices update 4 times per day during market hours. The data is delayed by approximately 15 minutes, which is standard for free market data.

The "What is this?" button at the top of the sector chart opens a guide explaining sectors, ETFs, and diversification in language a high school student can follow. No prior investing knowledge is assumed.
