# BLK Exchange
## Game Mechanics & How to Play
**Version:** 4.0 | **Date:** March 2026

---

## Overview

BLK Exchange is a cultural stock market simulator. You trade 36 fictional Black-economy companies using $10,000 in virtual capital. Prices only move when **events fire** — real cultural news from Black media publications, or AI-generated fictional headlines built around the companies in the game. Every event teaches a named investing concept. Every trade is a decision. Every session ends with a personalized AI debrief.

The goal is not just to make money. The goal is to understand **why** money moves.

## 1. Getting started

### Creating your account

Sign up via Clerk. No credit card, no real money, ever. Once signed in you receive:

- **$10,000** virtual starting capital
- Access to all 36 tickers immediately
- Your personal Knowledge Vault (empty at start, fills through play)
- A first-time tutorial overlay that walks you through the interface

Your portfolio resets to $10,000 at the start of each 8-week season. Your Knowledge Vault **never resets** — learning is permanent.

### Joining a classroom session

If your teacher has created a classroom session, tap **Join Session** on the home screen and enter the 6-digit code. You'll enter the shared market where the teacher controls which events fire and when.

## 2. The market

### The 36 tickers

BLK Exchange has 36 fictional companies across 12 sectors — 3 per sector. Every company is a cultural archetype grounded in Black economic reality.

| Sector | Tickers |

| Media & Content | LOUD, SCROLL, VERSE |
| Streaming | VIZN, NETFLO, LIVE |
| Music | RYTHM, BLOC, CRATE |
| Gaming | PIXL, MOBILE, SQUAD |
| Sportswear | KICKS, FLEX, COURT |
| Streetwear & Fashion | DRIP, RARE, THREAD |
| Publishing | INK, READS, PRESS |
| Beauty & Wellness | CROWN, GLOW, SHEEN |
| Finance & Banking | VAULT, STAX, GROW |
| Real Estate | BLOK, BUILD, HOOD |
| Sports & Athletics | DRAFT, ARENA, STATS |
| Entertainment | SCREEN, STAGE, GAME |

### Starting prices

Each ticker starts between $17 and $142. Prices are stored in cents internally to avoid floating-point errors. What you see on screen is the dollar display.

### How prices move

**Prices only move when an event fires.** Between events, everything is stable. This is intentional — it creates a clear rhythm:

```
Calm period → study the market → EVENT FIRES → prices jump
→ 60-second decision window → trade or hold → calm returns
```

This rhythm teaches the most important investing lesson before any concept is ever named: **disciplined investors study before the moment, not during it.**

### The BLK Index

The home screen shows a composite BLK Index — a weighted average of all 36 stocks. When a sector-wide event fires, you can watch the index move in real time. This models how real market indices (S&P 500, Dow Jones) respond to broad economic events.

## 3. How events work

### Two types of events

**Real news events** are sourced from 12 Black cultural publications — AfroTech, Essence, Black Enterprise, TheGrio, The Root, Blavity, HBCUBuzz, Andscape, Vibe, Rolling Out, EURweb, and Invest Fest. When a real headline is significant enough (scored 6+ out of 10 by the AI), it enters the event queue and fires during your session. These events are labeled in the news feed with the source publication name.

**Fictional events** are generated by Groq GPT-OSS 120B for the fictional companies. These are original business headlines — quarterly earnings, product launches, partnerships, competitive moves, personnel changes, macro impacts — written in the style of Black business media and grounded in each company's ongoing narrative. These are labeled **BLK Exchange News Desk**.

Both types of events are real in the context of the game. The distinction matters for one reason: transparency. Knowing whether a market signal comes from a primary source or a synthesized one is itself an investing skill.

### The event queue

The market fires **a maximum of 2 events per 10-minute window**. Events don't all pile in at once — they're paced to give you time to think, trade, and learn. During quiet periods, the AI generates events from the fictional event engine so the market never sits still for more than a few minutes.

### The Market Alert

When an event fires, a **Market Alert** slides in from the right side of the screen. It shows:

- The headline
- Which stocks moved and by how much (with green/red indicators)
- A 2-sentence plain-language explanation of what happened and why
- The investing concept this event teaches

ElevenLabs reads the Market Alert aloud automatically. You can toggle this off in settings.

### The 60-second decision window

After an event fires, a visible countdown timer appears. You have **60 seconds** to decide:

- **React** — buy or sell based on the event
- **Hold** — do nothing and let the market settle

After 60 seconds, prices lock at their new levels. The timer is visible to all players. This is the moment where emotional investing happens — and where the most important lessons get learned.

### Event impact on sectors

Events affect not just the directly mentioned company but adjacent stocks in related sectors. A boycott against a beauty brand doesn't just drop that stock — it shifts consumer dollars to competitors, moves sentiment across the sector, and sometimes affects the broader consumer spending index. These **correlation effects** are intentional and designed to teach sector dynamics.

## 4. Trading mechanics

### Market orders only (v1)

All trades in v1 are **market orders** — you buy or sell at the current price, immediately. No limit orders, stop-losses, or options in the first version. This is intentional. Market orders are how most people actually invest, and they create the clearest emotional lessons:

- You bought at the top → the price dropped → now you understand why timing is hard
- You sold at the bottom → the price recovered → now you understand why holding is hard

### Fractional shares

You trade by **dollar amount**, not whole shares. Type `$500 into KICKS` rather than `4.46 shares of KICKS`. This makes the $10,000 feel like a real budget to allocate — and directly mirrors how most modern investing apps work (Cash App, Robinhood, etc.).

### The trade flow

```
Tap any ticker
  → Detail panel opens
  → Tap BUY or SELL
  → Enter dollar amount
  → Preview shows: shares, execution price,
    new portfolio allocation %, new capital balance
  → Confirm
  → Convex mutation executes atomically
```

The atomic mutation means: your capital is validated, the trade is created, your holding is updated, and your balance is adjusted in a single operation. No partial state. If something fails, nothing changes.

### Trade execution speed

Trades execute instantly. There is no simulated market delay. Price impact (your trade moving the price) is not modeled in v1 — the market is the event engine, not individual trade volume.

## 5. Portfolio rules

### The 25% position limit

You cannot put more than **25% of your starting capital** ($2,500) into any single stock. If you try, the trade modal shows a warning and explains why:

> *"That would put 25% of your portfolio in one company. Concentration risk means one bad event wipes out a quarter of your wealth. Diversification isn't just a rule here — it's the lesson."*

This constraint is a teaching mechanism. Every time it fires, it delivers a financial literacy lesson without a lecture.

### No short selling

You can only **go long** — buy stocks you believe will rise. No shorting, no betting against companies. This keeps the game accessible and reinforces the wealth-building philosophy at the heart of BLK Exchange: we're here to own things.

Short selling is explained in the Knowledge Vault as a concept — you'll understand what it is, just won't do it here.

### No leverage or margin

No borrowed money, no margin accounts. You invest what you have. Again, this is explained in the vault — leverage is a concept worth knowing, but not a mechanic for a first-time investor.

### Starting capital: $10,000

$10,000 is psychologically meaningful — it's a real number you might actually save for investing. It's large enough to buy meaningful positions across multiple sectors, and small enough that every allocation decision matters.

### Bankruptcy mechanic

If your portfolio drops below **$1,000** (10% of starting capital), you receive a bankruptcy warning. You have two options:

1. **Keep playing** — try to recover from a depleted position
2. **Reset** — return to $10,000 (one reset allowed per season)

Choosing to reset unlocks a special Knowledge Vault card: *"Why diversification is the first rule of wealth preservation."* The bankruptcy mechanic isn't a punishment — it's a curriculum moment.

## 6. The education layer

### How concepts are taught

**Nothing is a lecture.** Every concept in the 23-concept curriculum is unlocked through a gameplay experience. You don't read about competitive displacement — you watch CROWN drop 11% and GLOW climb 4% in the same event, and then Claude explains what just happened in your portfolio.

### The Knowledge Vault

Your Knowledge Vault is a personal record of every investing concept you've experienced. It starts empty and fills as you play.

Each card contains:
- **Concept name and definition**
- **The moment you learned it** — the exact event headline that triggered the unlock
- **A real-world example** — how this concept applies outside the game
- **Source attribution** — if a real publication triggered the event, that source is credited

The vault is visible from the home screen with a progress bar: *"X of 23 investing concepts."*

### The curriculum — 23 concepts

**Foundation (5)** — first 1–2 sessions
- Supply and demand
- Bull and bear markets
- Buying and selling basics
- Profit and loss
- Portfolio value and net worth

**Intermediate (8)** — sessions 2–5
- Diversification
- Sector correlation
- Emotional vs disciplined investing
- Consumer spending power
- Economic multiplier effect
- Dividend investing
- Halo effect and brand association
- Competitive displacement

**Advanced (7)** — sessions 5–8
- Price-to-earnings ratio
- Economic moat
- Risk-adjusted return
- Dollar cost averaging
- Acquisition economics
- Venture capital and dilution
- Portfolio rebalancing

**Economics (3)** — any session
- Macroeconomics: inflation
- Macroeconomics: consumer confidence
- Community economics: the Black dollar

### The curriculum debt queue

After every session, the AI checks your Knowledge Vault against all 23 concepts. Any concept you haven't experienced yet goes into a **curriculum debt queue**. The next 1–3 events generated for your session are weighted toward clearing that debt.

This means: **no player finishes a season without experiencing all 23 concepts.** The guarantee isn't that you'll memorize them — it's that you'll *feel* each one through a trade decision before it's ever named.

### Portfolio Coach (Claude)

After every 3 events in a session, Claude grades your portfolio:

- **Diversification score** (0–100) based on sector spread and concentration
- **Concentration warnings** — if you're over 40% in one sector, Claude names it
- **One specific recommendation** — not generic advice, but a named stock and a reason

The portfolio coach uses your **actual holdings** as the example. If you're 65% in beauty stocks, Claude doesn't say "diversification is important." Claude says: *"CROWN, GLOW, and SHEEN account for 65% of your portfolio. That worked today because the sector ran up. Here's exactly what event would wipe that gain out."*

### Professor Mode (Claude)

Tap any stock, concept card, or Market Alert at any time and ask Claude anything. Professor mode uses your actual portfolio as the teaching context:

- *"Why did VAULT go up when interest rates rose?"*
- *"What's the difference between VIZN and NETFLO's business model?"*
- *"Should I sell RARE after the hype event?"*

Claude answers specifically — using your holdings, your gains/losses, and your vault progress as the example.

### The session debrief (Claude + ElevenLabs)

When you end a session, Claude generates a **personalized 400-word narrative**:

- What you did well
- What cost you money and why
- Three concepts you experienced this session, by name
- Your leaderboard position and trajectory
- One specific focus for your next session

ElevenLabs reads the opening paragraph aloud. The debrief is the most important moment in the product — it's where the gameplay experience converts into durable financial literacy.

### Shareable Vault cards

When you unlock a concept, you can share a card that shows:
- The concept name
- How much your portfolio gained (or lost) when you experienced it
- One-line investing insight

Designed for social sharing. The best financial education is peer-to-peer.

## 7. Session structure

### How long is a session?

A standard solo session runs **30–45 minutes**. It's designed to fit in a lunch break, a commute, or a class period.

### Minute-by-minute breakdown

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0:00 | Market opens. Prices are stable. News feed shows recent headlines. $10,000 available. |
| 0:30–2:00 | **Study phase.** Browse tickers, read company profiles, check recent events. No prices moving. This is pre-trade research. |
| ~2:00 | **First event fires.** 3–5 stocks move. Market Alert slides in. ElevenLabs reads the headline. 60-second timer starts. |
| 2:00–3:00 | **Decision window.** React (trade) or hold. Timer visible. |
| 3:00–8:00 | Prices stabilize at new levels. Concept badge may unlock. Portfolio P&L updates. |
| 8:00–25:00 | Events 2–5 fire in sequence, paced at max 2 per 10-minute window. Each event → alert → decision → coaching. After event 3, Claude grades your portfolio. |
| 25:00–35:00 | Market winds down. Review portfolio, check leaderboard, browse your Vault. |
| 35:00–45:00 | **Session ends.** Claude generates your personalized debrief. ElevenLabs reads the opening. Leaderboard updates. |

### Daily streak

Play 5 consecutive days and earn a **+$500 capital bonus** (5% boost). Play 10 consecutive days for a **+$1,000 bonus**. Play every day in a calendar week for a **bonus event** that fires exclusively for streak players.

Missing a day doesn't reset your streak — it just pauses it. The next day you play is Day 1 of a new streak. No shame, no punishment.

### Fresh $10,000 every season

At the start of each 8-week season, all portfolios reset to $10,000. Knowledge Vault cards are permanent — you keep everything you learned. Only the competition resets.

## 8. Leaderboards

Five boards, each rewarding a different dimension of investing skill.

### Portfolio Value Board

**What:** Total portfolio value vs $10,000 starting capital.
**Resets:** Every Monday.
**Teaches:** That returns matter — but weekly resets mean you're always competing fresh.

### Knowledge Vault Board

**What:** Most investing concepts unlocked.
**Resets:** Each 8-week season.
**Teaches:** That consistent learning across a season beats lucky one-session runs. The player who shows up every week and learns deliberately beats the player who had one great trade.

### Diversification Score Board

**What:** Claude-graded portfolio balance, 0–100. Measures sector spread, position concentration, and capital deployment.
**Resets:** Every Monday.
**Teaches:** That risk management is a competitive skill, not just a defensive one.

### Biggest Mover Board

**What:** Largest single-event portfolio gain.
**Resets:** After every major event.
**Teaches:** News anticipation. The players who study company states before events fire consistently top this board — not the players who react fastest.

### The Blueprint Award — Season Championship

**What:** 8-week composite score combining:
- Portfolio P&L over the season
- Knowledge Vault count at season close
- Average diversification score across the season

**Resets:** Every 8 weeks.
**Teaches:** That the best investors aren't just good traders — they learn consistently, manage risk, and stay in the game. Pure gamblers don't win The Blueprint Award.

The theme creates narrative arcs that make the season feel like a story you're investing inside, not just a market simulation.

### Portfolio reset at season end

- **Portfolio value** resets to $10,000
- **Knowledge Vault** cards are permanent — never reset
- **Leaderboard positions** reset

### Manual reset (mid-season)

One portfolio reset allowed per season. If you've burned your capital down, you can choose to restart at $10,000. This unlocks a special vault card: *"Why diversification is the first rule of wealth preservation."* The reset is not shame — it's a curriculum moment.

## 10. Classroom mode

### For teachers

Create a **classroom session** in 5 minutes:
1. Sign in and navigate to Classroom
2. Create a new session — this generates a **6-digit join code**
3. Share the code with students
4. Students join from any device, no app download required

### Teacher dashboard

Once students are connected, you have full control:

- **Event library** — browse all available events (real and fictional) and queue them manually
- **Manual trigger** — fire any event at any time, pacing the session to your lesson plan
- **Market pause/resume** — freeze prices while you're talking, resume when ready
- **Student grid** — live view of every student's portfolio value, diversification score, and vault count
- **Annotation panel** — add discussion prompts that appear on all student screens during events

### The classroom session flow (50-minute class period)

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0–5 min | Teacher explains BLK Exchange, students join via code and receive $10,000 |
| 5–10 min | Teacher triggers first event, walks the class through the Market Alert |
| 10–30 min | 2–3 more events, students trade and compete. Teacher monitors the student grid. |
| 30–40 min | Teacher pauses market. Class discusses: *What happened? Who traded well? Why?* |
| 40–50 min | Final event fires. Session debrief generates. Teacher reviews top 3 concepts with class. |

### Student debrief

At session end, every student receives their personal Claude debrief. The teacher sees a **class-level summary** showing:
- Top 3 concepts triggered in this session
- Class median portfolio P&L
- Individual student reports

### Homework mode

Teachers can assign solo sessions as homework. Convex tracks completion. The student's debrief from the homework session is available in the teacher dashboard the next day.

## 11. How BLK Exchange differs from other simulators

| | Investopedia | TickerSphere | MarketDraft | **BLK Exchange** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| What you trade | Real stocks | Real stocks | Real stocks / crypto | 36 fictional Black-economy companies |
| Price driver | Real market data | Real market data | Real market data | Cultural news + AI-generated events |
| Education model | Separate courses | Leaderboard competition | None | Concepts embedded in every event |
| Cultural context | None | None | None | Black business media, cultural calendar |
| AI coaching | None | None | None | Claude portfolio coach, professor mode, debrief |
| Voice narration | None | None | None | ElevenLabs reads every market alert and debrief |
| Curriculum guarantee | None | None | None | 23-concept curriculum debt system |
| Session length | Open-ended | Daily/weekly | 1 day–1 month | 30–45 minutes |
| Classroom mode | Via MarketWatch | No | No | Full teacher dashboard, event control |

### The key differences

**Prices move differently.** In every other simulator, prices change continuously based on real market data. In BLK Exchange, prices are stable between events and only move when something culturally significant happens. This creates a learning rhythm that continuous markets cannot provide.

**The education is inside the game, not alongside it.** Every other platform separates learning from playing — there's a simulator, and there are lessons. In BLK Exchange, the lesson fires in the same moment the price moves. You feel the concept before you know its name.

**The companies are culturally grounded.** Trading fictional Black-economy companies that reflect real cultural archetypes (community banks, natural hair brands, Black-owned streaming platforms) makes the investing concepts concrete in a way that trading Apple or Tesla never could for this audience.

**The curriculum is guaranteed.** Every other simulator teaches only what the real market happens to deliver. BLK Exchange uses a curriculum debt queue — if you haven't experienced sector correlation in 3 sessions, the AI generates an event specifically designed to teach it. No player finishes a season without a complete financial literacy education.
