# LinkedIn Post: Sector Performance Feature

## Post (attach the infographic image)

New learning feature in BLK Exchange, a stock market simulator that teaches financial literacy through 36 fictional Black-economy companies: every sector in the sim is now mapped to a real ETF on Wall Street.

Tap Entertainment and you see your sim stocks (SCREEN, STAGE, GAME) next to the real Invesco Leisure & Entertainment ETF at $57.87. A visual bar shows how both moved today. The sim is flat. The real market is up 3.5%. That gap is a conversation.

12 sectors. 12 real ETFs. Finance maps to XLF. Music maps to MUSQ. Gaming maps to HERO. Each dropdown shows the sim stocks, real ETF price, top 5 holdings, and a plain-language explanation.

When a student watches their sim Finance sector drop on the same day JPMorgan drops, they are not reading about sector correlation. They are watching it happen.

I built this because the biggest gap in financial literacy education is the bridge between the classroom and the real market. This feature makes that bridge visible.

If you teach financial literacy or run a youth program, how would you use this?
