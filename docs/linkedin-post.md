For every $100 in wealth a White family holds, a Black family holds $15.

I kept reading that stat and waiting for someone to build the right tool. Nobody did. So last week I built it myself.

BLK Exchange is a stock market simulator where Black culture IS the economy. You trade 36 fictional companies: CROWN (hair care). KICKS (footwear). DRIP (streetwear). VIZN (streaming). VAULT (community bank). Real news from 16 Black media publications moves the prices. AI coaches you through 23 investing concepts as you play.

You don't read a textbook. You learn by trading companies you actually care about.

I did the research first. Game-based learning works. Culturally responsive education produces better outcomes for Black students. No existing platform combines both with a structured investing curriculum. That gap is what I built for.

Here's the part that still surprises me: only 8 Black-owned companies are publicly traded on major U.S. exchanges. Eight. A feature in development will introduce those real companies into the sim. Urban One. Carver Bancorp. Broadway Financial. The NAACP Minority Empowerment ETF as the graduation capstone. From sim to real.

Just submitted to the Hackonomics 2026 hackathon. The app is live and free.
