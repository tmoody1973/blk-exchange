# BLK Exchange: Institutional Adoption Requirements

## What Organizations Need to License and Incorporate BLK Exchange Into Their Programs

This document outlines what the NAACP, Earn Your Leisure, Operation HOPE, school districts, HBCUs, community organizations, and financial institutions would need from BLK Exchange before incorporating it into their programming.

Written from the perspective of program directors, educators, and organizational leaders who evaluate tools for their communities.

---

## Who Would License BLK Exchange

### Tier 1: National Organizations
- **NAACP** — Empowerment Programs arm, Financial Empowerment Month, Youth Programs, local branch workshops
- **Earn Your Leisure** — EYL University (12,000+ members), Invest Fest, Operation Crossroads youth program
- **Operation HOPE** — HOPE Inside for Youth, Financial Wellbeing Coaches, two-generational programming

### Tier 2: Educational Institutions
- **HBCUs** — Business departments, freshman orientation, campus financial literacy requirements
- **High schools** — Personal finance courses (26 states now require financial literacy for graduation)
- **School districts** — District-wide adoption across multiple schools

### Tier 3: Community Organizations
- **Churches** — Youth ministry financial literacy programs
- **Community development organizations** — CDFI-adjacent financial education
- **Libraries** — Public library financial literacy programming
- **Boys & Girls Clubs, YMCAs** — After-school enrichment

### Tier 4: Financial Institutions
- **Black-owned banks** (Carver, Citizens Trust, Broadway Financial) — Customer education
- **Credit unions** — Member financial literacy (similar to Banzai's model with bank sponsorship)
- **Wealth management firms** — Client onboarding for new investors

## What Each Organization Type Needs

### What the NAACP Would Need

The NAACP advocates at federal, state, and local levels to create financial literacy programs for African American children in K-12. They collaborate with faith-based groups and community organizations. Their local branches run workshops with banks.

**Requirements:**
1. **White-label capability** — NAACP branding on the platform alongside BLKX. "Powered by BLK Exchange" with NAACP logo.
2. **Branch-level deployment** — each NAACP branch gets their own instance or class code. National can see aggregate data. Branch directors see local data.
3. **NACP ETF integration** — the NAACP co-designed the NACP Minority Empowerment ETF. BLK Exchange already has this as the capstone. This is a direct alignment point.
4. **Impact reporting** — aggregate data showing how many youth participated, concepts unlocked, sessions completed, portfolio performance. For NAACP Impact Reports and Annual Convention presentations.
5. **Financial Empowerment Month toolkit** — ready-made 4-week curriculum that branches can deploy in April without designing their own.
6. **No data liability** — NAACP would need BLK Exchange to carry the data liability, not the branch. Data Processing Agreement required.

### What Earn Your Leisure Would Need

EYL started in classrooms (Troy Millings is an educator). They run Operation Crossroads for teens, EYL University for adults, and Invest Fest for thousands. Their audience is 3M+ across platforms.

**Requirements:**
1. **Co-branded experience** — "BLK Exchange x EYL" with EYL branding, possibly EYL-themed events or tickers
2. **EYL University integration** — embed BLK Exchange as the hands-on practice layer within EYL University's 200+ webinar library. After watching an EYL webinar on diversification, the student practices it in BLK Exchange.
3. **Invest Fest activation** — a live competition at Invest Fest where attendees build portfolios in real-time and compete on leaderboards during the event. Top performers win prizes.
4. **Audience funnel** — EYL drives users to BLK Exchange. BLK Exchange drives users back to EYL content. Mutual referral.
5. **Content creator tools** — EYL hosts should be able to create custom challenges tied to their episodes. "This week Rashad talked about P/E ratios. Your challenge: find the stock with the best P/E in the sim."
6. **Revenue share** — EYL would want a revenue share or licensing fee structure that aligns with their business model. They would not adopt a tool they cannot monetize.

### What Operation HOPE Would Need

Operation HOPE delivers programming through Financial Wellbeing Coaches in banks, workplaces, schools, and disaster recovery sites. They use the HOPE in Hand app for mobile access.

**Requirements:**
1. **Coach dashboard** — a Financial Wellbeing Coach needs to see all their clients' progress in one view. Similar to the teacher dashboard but framed as coach-to-client.
2. **Integration with HOPE in Hand app** — either embed BLK Exchange within their existing app or deep-link from HOPE in Hand to BLK Exchange.
3. **Two-generational tracking** — a coach working with a family needs to see both the teen's progress and the parent's progress. BLK Exchange's Parents & Educators guide supports this conceptually, but the platform needs family accounts.
4. **Financial Wellbeing Score** — Operation HOPE tracks outcomes via their Financial Wellness Index and Community Credit Score Index. BLK Exchange needs an equivalent composite score that maps to HOPE's framework.
5. **Disaster recovery mode** — a simplified version for disaster recovery settings where people are under stress and need basic financial concepts, not a full sim.
6. **Certification** — upon completing the 23 concepts, the user gets a certificate that HOPE can include in their client outcomes reporting. A PDF or shareable credential.

### What a High School Teacher Would Need

A teacher responsible for a financial literacy course with 30 students who need to show measurable outcomes to their principal and enter grades.

**Requirements:**
1. **Classroom mode with class codes** — teacher creates a class, gets a join code, students enter the code with a username. No student email required. No individual Clerk accounts.
2. **Teacher dashboard** — view all students at a glance: portfolio value, vault progress, trades this week, session count, streak. Color-coded: green = on track, yellow = falling behind, red = inactive.
3. **Progress reports (exportable)** — CSV or PDF per student showing: concepts unlocked (mapped to standards), portfolio change, trades executed, session time, discussion participation. This goes in the gradebook.
4. **Assignment system** — teacher assigns weekly goals inside the app: "Make 5 trades this week" or "Unlock 2 new concepts" or "Hold stocks in 4+ sectors." Progress tracked automatically.
5. **Structured challenges** — pre-built scenarios: "CROWN announced a product recall. Your portfolio has 25% in Beauty. What do you do?" Students make decisions, AI grades the response.
6. **Vocabulary quizzes** — test comprehension of glossary terms. Multiple choice or matching, auto-graded, scores exportable.
7. **Content controls** — disable AI chat (some schools block AI), disable news feed (content concerns), disable real company references (sim-only mode).
8. **FERPA compliance** — Data Processing Agreement template. Student data minimization. Automatic data deletion at end of semester.
9. **Standards alignment report** — show which Jump$tart, CEE, or state standards each vault concept maps to. This justifies the tool to curriculum directors.

### What an HBCU Would Need

A university business department incorporating BLK Exchange into freshman-level Personal Finance or Introduction to Investing courses.

**Requirements:**
1. **LMS integration** — embed BLK Exchange assignments within Canvas, Blackboard, or Moodle. Grade passback via LTI.
2. **Semester-long tracking** — 16-week view of student progress, not just weekly.
3. **Research data access** — anonymized, aggregate data for faculty research on financial literacy outcomes. IRB-compatible data export.
4. **Student portfolio showcase** — at semester end, students present their BLK Exchange portfolio as a final project. Need exportable portfolio summary with all trades, concepts, and AI debrief excerpts.
5. **Real Exchange readiness** — HBCU students are closer to actual investing age. The Real Exchange feature (8 real Black-owned companies) is a strong differentiator for this audience.
6. **Campus competition** — inter-dorm or inter-class leaderboard competitions. Modeled on the existing 5 leaderboards but scoped to the university.

### What a Financial Institution Would Need

A Black-owned bank or credit union offering financial literacy to customers or members.

**Requirements:**
1. **Custom branding** — bank logo and colors alongside BLK Exchange
2. **Customer onboarding flow** — after completing 15+ concepts in the sim, prompt to open a real account at the sponsoring bank
3. **CRA credit documentation** — banks that sponsor financial literacy programs can receive Community Reinvestment Act credit. Need documentation of program outcomes.
4. **No competitor references** — the sim should not promote other banks or brokerages. Content controls to remove references to specific real financial institutions.
5. **API for outcomes data** — the bank's CRM needs to receive completion data (e.g., "Customer completed 23 concepts on [date]")

## Pricing Model for Institutional Licensing

| Tier | Audience | Price | Includes |
|------|----------|-------|----------|
| **Free** | Individual users | $0 | Full sim, 36 tickers, 23 concepts, AI coach, news |
| **Classroom** | Teachers (up to 5 classes, 150 students) | $0 (free for educators) | Class codes, teacher dashboard, progress reports, assignments |
| **Organization** | Nonprofits, churches, community orgs | $50/month | 10 classes, org dashboard, impact reports, certificates |
| **Enterprise** | School districts, national orgs, banks | Custom pricing | Unlimited classes, white-label, SSO, API, dedicated support |

**The Banzai model works here:** Banzai offers financial literacy courses free to teachers, sponsored by local banks and credit unions who pay for the branding opportunity. BLK Exchange could offer the Classroom tier free, funded by bank/credit union sponsorships who get co-branding + CRA credit.

## Competitive Positioning

| Platform | What It Does | What BLK Exchange Does Differently |
|----------|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Banzai** | Free financial literacy courses sponsored by banks | BLK Exchange is a market simulation, not a course. Learning happens through trading, not reading. |
| **Investopedia Simulator** | Trade real stocks with fake money | BLK Exchange uses culturally relevant fictional companies. 36 tickers in 12 sectors of the Black economy. |
| **Goalsetter** | Game-based financial literacy for kids | Goalsetter teaches saving. BLK Exchange teaches investing. Different skill, different audience. |
| **EVERFI** | Digital financial literacy courses for schools | EVERFI is curriculum delivery. BLK Exchange is a simulation with AI coaching. More engaging, less passive. |
| **Next Gen Personal Finance** | Free teacher curriculum + resources | NGPF provides lesson plans. BLK Exchange provides the hands-on practice tool that NGPF lessons prepare students for. |
| **Stock Market Game (SIFMA)** | Trade real stocks in classroom competitions | Generic, culturally neutral. No AI coaching. No curriculum guarantee. No cultural relevance. |

**BLK Exchange is the only platform that combines:**
1. Culturally relevant fictional companies (not Apple and Tesla)
2. AI coaching tied to actual portfolio behavior
3. Structured curriculum with guaranteed concept exposure
4. Real cultural news driving the market
5. Real S&P 500 sector ETF comparison for educational context
6. Research backing with 28 peer-reviewed citations
